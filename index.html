About

DIETZESTHUB was set up in 2015 by registered dietitian, Denise McCarthy.

Denise is passionate about helping people achieve their health and nutrition goals, her philosophy is simple: plan for health.

Denise

So what makes Denise qualified to help you?


Qualifications:


  • BA in Home Economics (1992) from Queen's University, Glasgow
  • BSc in Human Nutrition and Dietetics (2005) from Trinity College Dublin

Denise has over 18 years’ post-graduate experience as a registered dietitian and is passionate about helping people achieve their health and nutrition goals.


As senior dietitian in one of the main teaching hospitals in Dublin, Denise ran various clinics on heart health, weight management, insulin therapy and diabetes, chronic kidney disease, renal replacement therapy, coeliac disease, eating disorders and low FODMAP diets. She also has experience in providing nutrition support for patients with neurodisability such as autism and Down’s syndrome.


Denise’s passion for good food and nutrition stems from her personal challenge with chronic fatigue syndrome (ME) while studying for her undergraduate degree in home economics. The journey to a complete recovery took two years, six months of which in bed, which helped Denise empathise with the struggle that goes with achieving good health.


Whether supporting children with food allergies and fussy or aversive eating or promoting positive ageing for women through nutrition, Denise’s vast experience through the lifespan is guided by a simple philosophy: plan for health.


Sinead

So what makes Sinead qualified to help you?


Qualifications:


  • BSc Nutrition & Dietetics, Trinity College Dublin and Dublin Institute of Technology
  • EU Basics in Public Health Nutrition, Karolinska Institute, Sweden
  • Additional training in behaviour change, nutrition & women’s health including women’s fertility, Intuitive Eating and low FODMAP diet
  • Paediatrics Nutrition: General children’s health including allergies and fussy eaters

Sinead has 18 years of post-graduate experience as a dietitian. Her appetite for evidence-based learning and passion for nutrition continue to underpin her individual dietetic practice.


Sinead has vast experience in diabetes, cholesterol lowering diets, IBS and weight management. In addition, she partners with allied health colleagues to offer rehab dietetics for older people.


However, her specialist areas of interest include menopause and women’s health, particularly after experiencing menopause symptoms herself following a hysterectomy in 2019. Both her professional and personal experiences motivate Sinead to research and practise in this evolving area.


Whether working in a clinical setting, in health promotion or in industry, Sinead places patient needs at the centre of her practice.
