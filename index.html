Plan For Health

About

HappyZest Nutrition was set up in 2015 by registered dietitian, Denise McCarthy.

Denise is passionate about helping people achieve their health and nutrition goals, her philosophy is simple: plan for health.

So what makes Denise qualified to help you?


Qualifications:

  • BA in Home Economics (1992) from Queen's University, Glasgow
  • BSc in Human Nutrition and Dietetics (2005) from Trinity College Dublin

My Home Economics degree, a three-year course, took me five years to complete because I suffered from chronic fatigue syndrome (ME), or ‘yuppie flu’ as it was commonly known at the time. The journey to a complete recovery took two years (of which six months was spent in bed), but it ultimately came down to three simple things: meditation, good nutrition and rest. This period of my life helped me value health and ignited a passion for good food and nutrition. It also helped me empathise with the struggle that goes with achieving good health


Experience:

  • Over 16years experience working as a registered dietitian ,10 of which in one of the main teaching hospitals in Dublin, managing the outpatient nutrition department and running various clinics on heart health, weight loss, weight gain, insulin therapy and diabetes, chronic kidney disease, renal replacement therapy, coeliac disease, eating disorders and low FODMAP for IBS and IBD.
  • Pediatric Dietitian : Neurodisability , Aversive feeding and General children’s health including allergies and fussy eaters
  • Women’s health: over 7 years working as a private dietitian to promote positive aging through nutrition.

Services

At HappyZest Nutrition we offer a diverse range of services, with an overall committment to:


  • teach you the correct mindset to achieve your lifestyle or medical-nutrition goals.
  • remove jargon from any medical-nutrition related issue.
  • provide only evidence-based advice.
  • teach you how ‘failing‘ is part of the plan.
  • share practical tips, recipes and meal ideas for a wide range of conditions and needs.
